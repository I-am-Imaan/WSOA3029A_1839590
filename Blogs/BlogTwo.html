UI/UX Analysis Of Superbalist

Superbalist is a South African online shopping website. They sell a wide variety of fashion products, ranging from clothing, shoes, accessories, beauty products, as well as homeware for men, women, and children. Superbalist is a very popular website and is used by many South Africans nationwide. In this blogpost, I will be critically analyzing the homepage of their website in terms of its UI, UX and general design – as well as the various forms of representation presented on and through their homepage.

The Superbalist homepage makes use of and implements quite a few UI elements onto its homepage. The homepage contains interactable buttons that aid in site navigation, dropdown menu’s, search and input fields, as well as various icons. The UI of the homepage is simple and clean, elements are consistent, the page layout has been well thought out and communicated, and the colour and typography used in the homepage design is readable and minimalistic . The Superbalist website uses a colour palette consisting of black, white and greens. There is not an overwhelming amount of colour present on the homepage and the use of green is implemented in order to catch the viewer’s attention and emphasize important aspects of the website (such as sales etc.). The homepage layout is done in such a way that guides the user through pages for clothing specified to their gender and age group.

In terms of representation and the construction of meaning through their selected UI elements, Superbalist makes use of icons such as shopping carts, delivery vehicles etc. These icons have attached meanings that have been constructed in relation to their associations. The shopping cart is associated with the physical cart used in shopping malls, and allows users to make a connection between the similar activities of shopping in person to that of shopping online. Their choice of the order of how categories are presented, also have meaning and implications. The women’s category is always placed before the men’s and is also emphasized more. The dropdown menu for the women’s category also has a significantly larger amount of options, in comparison to the men’s dropdown menu. This suggests the constructed assumption and representation of women enjoying and partaking in shopping more often than men. This speaks to the presentational context of the website and reveals the intentions of Superbalist as a brand.
